## Section 2 — Cross-Course Relationship Map

Use this to identify when a request in one domain must draw context from another course.

| Relationship | Type | Files Involved | Why It Matters |
|-------------|------|---------------|----------------|
| Value Equation ↔ Lead Magnets | Foundational | Offers/04 ↔ Leads/03 | Magnets must score high on Value Equation or they don't convert; the 4-factor formula governs magnet design |
| Value Equation ↔ Pricing | Justification | Offers/04 ↔ Offers/03 | Higher Value Equation score justifies higher price; pricing psychology only works when value is established first |
| CAC ↔ Pricing | Constraint | Money/03 ↔ Offers/03 | Price must exceed CAC over the payback window; CAC sets the floor, pricing sets the ceiling |
| CAC ↔ Paid Ads | Measurement | Money/03 ↔ Leads/08-09 | Ads are the primary CAC lever; you cannot optimize ads without understanding CAC math |
| CAC ↔ Open To Goal | Budget | Money/03 ↔ Leads/15 | CAC determines how much budget to allocate to each channel in Open To Goal planning |
| Payback Period ↔ Continuity | LTV Extension | Money/05 ↔ Money/26 | Continuity (subscriptions) extends LTV, which directly shortens effective payback period and allows higher CAC |
| Continuity ↔ Referrals | Loyalty Effect | Money/26 ↔ Leads/11 | Subscribers refer more than one-time buyers; referral program design should target continuity customers first |
| Attraction Offers ↔ Lead Magnets | Vocabulary | Money/10 ↔ Leads/03 | These are the same concept described in different contexts: lead magnets (Leads course) = attraction offers (Money course) |
| Offer Stacks ↔ Offer Creation | Scope | Money/07 ↔ Offers/05-06 | Offer Creation designs individual offer components; Offer Stacks arranges those components into the full monetization system |
| 4 Types of Offers ↔ Offer Creation | Taxonomy | Money/08 ↔ Offers/05-06 | The 4-type taxonomy classifies where each offer sits in the stack; Offer Creation explains how to build each type |
| Guarantees ↔ Value Equation | PLA Component | Offers/08 ↔ Offers/04 | Guarantees directly increase Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (PLA), the second factor in the Value Equation |
| Guarantees ↔ Win Your Money Back | Strength Spectrum | Offers/08 ↔ Money/11 | Win Your Money Back is the strongest guarantee type; Guarantees covers the full spectrum |
| Upsells ↔ Offer Stacks | Placement | Money/17-21 ↔ Money/07 | Upsells are one layer of the stack; you need Offer Stacks context to understand where upsells fit and when to trigger them |
| Cold Outreach ↔ Offer Creation | Message Payload | Leads/07 ↔ Offers/05 | Cold outreach only works if you have a compelling offer to put in the message; the two must be designed together |
| Cold Outreach ↔ First Customers | Sequencing | Leads/07 ↔ Leads/04 | First 5 clients almost always come through direct outreach; these two are designed to be read together |
| Scaling Framework ↔ Open To Goal | Growth Logic | Leads/10 ↔ Leads/15 | Open To Goal sets targets; More/Better/New is the framework for deciding HOW to hit those targets at scale |
| Affiliates ↔ Referrals | Formalization | Leads/14 ↔ Leads/11 | Referrals are informal; affiliates are the formalized, incentivized version of the same channel |
| Bonuses ↔ Offer Creation | Perceived Value | Offers/07 ↔ Offers/05 | Bonuses are a component of offer creation; cannot design bonuses without understanding the core offer structure first |
| Product Naming ↔ Offer Creation | Packaging | Offers/10 ↔ Offers/05 | Names are the packaging layer on top of the offer; naming should happen after offer construction, not before |

## Section 3 — Concept Clusters

Named file groups that should be loaded together. The CONTEXT-STRATEGIST selects from these clusters — do not split clusters arbitrarily.

When a cluster has more than 4 files, select the first-listed files (highest priority) unless the user's request specifically points to later files.

### `PRICING_STRATEGY`
**Files:** Offers/03, Offers/04, Money/03, Money/05
**Use when:** user asks what to charge, how to price a SaaS product, price anchoring, whether their pricing is correct, premium vs commodity positioning
**Cross-course note:** Pricing requires all 3 courses — psychology (Offers), cost floor (Money/CAC), and timing (Money/Payback)

### `OFFER_DESIGN`
**Files:** Offers/04, Offers/05, Offers/06, Money/07, Money/08
**Use when:** user is building a product or service structure, designing what they sell, figuring out offer components, positioning their solution
**Cross-course note:** Offers/04 is foundational; Money/07-08 adds the system context (where does this offer fit in the full stack)

### `ACQUISITION_SYSTEM`
**Files:** Leads/15, Leads/16, Leads/10, Money/03
**Use when:** user needs a complete acquisition strategy, lead gen planning, figuring out which channels to prioritize, growth roadmap
**Cross-course note:** Money/03 (CAC) is included because channel selection must be informed by cost math

### `FIRST_CUSTOMERS`
**Files:** Leads/04, Offers/02, Offers/05, Money/10
**Use when:** user has 0 customers or is pre-revenue, needs early traction, no audience or brand yet
**Cross-course note:** Offer/02 (market selection) is critical — most Stage 0 failures are picking the wrong market, not the wrong tactic

### `CONTENT_ENGINE`
**Files:** Leads/05, Leads/06, Leads/10
**Use when:** user wants organic content strategy, social media growth, publishing cadence, content system
**Note:** Leads/10 (More/Better/New) is the scaling framework for content once the engine is running

### `PAID_ACQUISITION`
**Files:** Leads/08, Leads/09, Money/03, Money/05
**Use when:** user is running or planning paid ads (Meta, Google, TikTok, YouTube), wants to scale paid acquisition
**Cross-course note:** Money/03 and Money/05 are required context — ads without CAC and payback math lead to unprofitable scaling

### `REFERRAL_NETWORK`
**Files:** Leads/11, Leads/14, Leads/10
**Use when:** user wants word-of-mouth systems, referral programs, affiliate programs, partner channel
**Note:** Leads/14 (Affiliates) is the formalized version — if user asks about both referrals and affiliates, load both

### `SUBSCRIPTION_MODEL`
**Files:** Money/26, Money/27, Money/28, Offers/03
**Use when:** user is designing a subscription product, SaaS pricing tiers, membership model, recurring revenue
**Cross-course note:** Offers/03 (Charge Its Worth) needed for continuity-specific pricing psychology

### `UPSELL_ARCHITECTURE`
**Files:** Money/07, Money/17, Money/18, Money/19, Money/20
**Use when:** user is building upsell flows, post-purchase expansion, in-cart offers, cross-sell sequences
**Note:** Money/07 (Offer Stacks) must be loaded first — it provides the system context for where upsells live

### `RISK_REVERSAL`
**Files:** Offers/08, Offers/09, Money/11
**Use when:** user wants to reduce buyer friction, needs guarantee strategy, working on conversion rate at point of sale
**Note:** Offers/09 (Scarcity/Urgency) pairs with guarantees — risk reversal + urgency is the full conversion toolkit

### `CHANNEL_STRATEGY`
**Files:** Leads/12, Leads/13, Leads/14, Leads/16
**Use when:** user wants to scale through employees, agencies, or affiliates as distribution channels; multi-channel design
**Note:** Leads/16 (Roadmap) provides sequencing logic for combining channels

### `FINANCIAL_ARCHITECTURE`
**Files:** Money/02, Money/03, Money/04, Money/06, Money/31
**Use when:** user is designing business model, modeling unit economics, fundraising, calculating sustainability of a pricing/CAC combination, long-term strategy
**Note:** Money/31 (Ten Years Ten Minutes) is the long-view LTV compounding framework — include for strategic planning requests
